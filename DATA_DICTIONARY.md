# DimensionsPot - BodySize Engine: **Data Dictionary** 

<br>

Complete reference of all **130 dimensions** returned by the DimensionsPot API (`POST /v1/predict`) when `bundle: "FULL_BODY"` is requested. Dimensions are grouped by anatomical region; each section header identifies the bundle filter that returns that group.

> **BONE** = structural/skeletal dimension, measured at bony landmarks; lower variability across body positions.  
> **FLESH** = soft tissue dimension, dependent on body composition and posture.  
> All linear values in **mm** (metric) or **inch** (imperial). `body_mass` in **kg** or **lb**.  
> **†** — dimension is returned by two bundle filters; see bundle notes in the section header.  
> **‡** — Full Body only; not returned by any named bundle filter. Always available via `FULL_BODY`.

---

## Head, Face & Neck — 24 dimensions

Bundle filter: `"bundle": "HEAD_FACE"` returns the first 20 dimensions below.  
`neck_circumference` is returned by both `HEAD_FACE` and `TORSO` filters (†).  
The four ear and head-height dimensions (‡) are returned by `FULL_BODY` only.

| API Key | Label | Type | ISO 7250-1:2017 | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `bigonial_breadth` | Jaw Breadth | BONE | — | Maximum horizontal breadth of the lower jaw across the gonial angles (mandibular corners). Determines the widest point of the lower face. Used in surgical mask and respirator sizing (lower face seal), and in face recognition applications. |
| `bitragion_chin_arc` | Bitragion Chin Arc | BONE | — | Surface arc from the right tragion (ear canal opening) across the chin to the left tragion. The shortest of the bitragion arc series — covers the lower face contour. Used for chin-strap length in helmets and face shields, and for face mask pattern making. |
| `bitragion_coronal_arc` | Bitragion Coronal Arc | BONE | — | Surface arc from the right tragion, across the crown of the head, to the left tragion. The longest of the bitragion arcs — spans the full vertical head profile. Critical for helmet liner and padded headgear pattern construction, VR head strap tensioning. |
| `bitragion_frontal_arc` | Bitragion Frontal Arc | BONE | — | Surface arc from the right tragion, across the forehead, to the left tragion. Slightly shorter than the coronal arc. Used for headband and forehead-strap sizing in AR/VR headsets, mining lamps, and head-mounted displays. |
| `bitragion_subnasale_arc` | Bitragion Subnasale Arc | BONE | — | Surface arc from the right tragion, under the nose, to the left tragion. Covers the mid-face region between the coronal and chin arcs. Used in respirator face-piece sizing and custom face-shield pattern making. |
| `bizygomatic_breadth` | Cheekbone Breadth | BONE | — | Maximum horizontal breadth of the face across the zygomatic arches (cheekbones). The widest point of the face. Primary dimension for face shape classification and for determining eyeglass frame width relative to face width. Typical adult range: 130–155 mm. |
| `bridge_width` | Bridge Width | BONE | — | Horizontal width of the nose bridge at the nasal root (sellion), with standard 2 mm nose pad contact correction applied (total −4 mm from `nasal_root_breadth`). Industry standard for eyeglass frame fitting — determines bridge size in frame specifications. Typical range: 14–22 mm. See also: `nasal_root_breadth`. |
| `face_length` | Face Length | BONE | 6.3.11 | Vertical distance from the sellion (nose root) to the menton (chin). Identical measurement to `menton_sellion_length` — both carry ISO 6.3.11; the two keys are retained as commercial aliases. Used for face shield height, balaclava sizing, and face mask length. Typical adult range: 110–130 mm. |
| `head_breadth` | Head Breadth | BONE | 6.3.10 | Maximum lateral breadth of the head measured above the ears (euryon to euryon). Together with `head_length` and `head_circumference`, one of the three primary head sizing dimensions. Determines helmet shell width. Typical adult range: 145–165 mm. |
| `head_circumference` | Head Circumference | FLESH | 6.3.12 | Maximum horizontal circumference of the head, measured above the eyebrows and over the occipital protrusion. The universal basis for hat sizing: ISO hat size = circumference in cm (e.g., size 58 = 58 cm). Also used for helmet, bicycle, motorcycle, and industrial hard-hat sizing. Adult population average: ~57 cm (male) / ~55 cm (female). |
| `head_length` | Head Length | BONE | 6.3.9 | Anteroposterior head depth from the glabella (mid-forehead) to the most posterior point of the skull (occiput). Together with `head_breadth`, defines the cephalic index (head shape ratio). Critical for helmet shell front-to-back sizing. Typical adult range: 180–210 mm. |
| `interpupillary_distance` | Interpupillary Distance | BONE | — | Horizontal distance between the optical centres of the left and right pupils. The primary parameter for lens centration in prescription eyewear — determines whether optical centres align with the wearer's visual axes. Also defines the default IPD setting in VR headsets. Population range: 57–72 mm, average ~63 mm (male) / ~60 mm (female). |
| `lip_length` | Lip Length | BONE | — | Horizontal distance between the left and right cheilion (mouth corners). Determines mouth width for face mask aperture sizing, custom mouthguard fitting, and singing/wind instrument mouthpiece design. Typical adult range: 45–58 mm. |
| `maximum_frontal_breadth` | Max Frontal Breadth | BONE | — | Maximum horizontal breadth of the forehead at its widest point (above the temporal lines). Broader than `minimum_frontal_breadth`. Used for forehead-band sizing in medical devices, hard hat comfort bands, and head-mounted camera mounts. |
| `menton_sellion_length` | Face Length (Menton-Sellion) | BONE | 6.3.11 | Craniometric alias for `face_length` — identical measurement (sellion to menton). Both keys are returned in the response and carry the same value. Retained to support customers using either naming convention from anatomical or anthropometric literature. |
| `minimum_frontal_breadth` | Min Frontal Breadth | BONE | — | Minimum horizontal breadth of the forehead, measured between the temporal crests (narrowest point above the temples). Together with `maximum_frontal_breadth`, characterises forehead shape. Used in eyewear temple-width calculation and sports goggle lens sizing. |
| `nasal_root_breadth` | Nasal Root Breadth | BONE | — | Horizontal breadth of the nose at the level of the nasal root (sellion), measured at the inner corners of the eyes. Raw anatomical measurement without nose-pad correction. Used in AR/VR nose-rest design where 3D surface contact geometry matters. See also: `bridge_width` (pad-corrected value for eyeglass frames). |
| `neck_circumference` † | Neck Circumference | FLESH | 6.4.9 | Horizontal circumference of the neck below the laryngeal prominence (Adam's apple). Returned by both `HEAD_FACE` and `TORSO` bundle filters. Used for collar sizing in shirts (dress shirt size is neck circumference in inches or cm), neck brace fitting, and protective neck-guard sizing. Typical adult range: 340–440 mm (male), 300–380 mm (female). |
| `nose_breadth` | Nose Breadth | BONE | — | Maximum horizontal breadth across the alar wings of the nose (widest nostril point). Used in respirator and face mask sizing to determine lateral seal width, and in custom nose-clip design for swimming goggles. |
| `subnasale_sellion_length` | Nose Length | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the subnasale (base of the nose columella) to the sellion (nose root). Defines the vertical nose profile height. Used for nose-rest and bridge height in eyeglass frames, surgical mask nose-bridge wire sizing, and VR headset nose clearance. |
| `ear_breadth` ‡ | Ear Breadth | BONE | — | Maximum horizontal breadth of the auricle from the helix to the antihelix. Used in custom hearing aid shell sizing and in-ear audio device fitting. Together with `ear_length`, defines the ear morphology for behind-the-ear (BTE) hearing aid retention arm design. |
| `ear_length` ‡ | Ear Length | BONE | — | Maximum vertical length of the auricle from the top of the helix to the bottom of the lobule. Primary dimension for custom ear mould sizing. Typical adult range: 55–75 mm. Used in earbud and hearing aid product sizing charts alongside `ear_breadth`. |
| `ear_protrusion` ‡ | Ear Protrusion | BONE | — | Horizontal distance from the side of the head (temporal surface) to the most lateral point of the helix. Determines how far the ear projects from the head. Used in helmet liner design (ear cup depth clearance) and earphone cushion-height selection. Typical range: 15–30 mm. |
| `tragion_top_of_head` ‡ | Tragion-Top of Head | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the tragion (ear canal opening, tragus cartilage) to the vertex (crown of the head). Used as a height reference in helmet shell sizing, particularly for equestrian and combat helmets where internal crown height determines fit class. |

## Torso & Trunk — 38 dimensions

Bundle filter: `"bundle": "TORSO"` returns the first 28 dimensions below.  
`knee_height`, `popliteal_height`, and `trochanterion_height` (†) are also returned by the `LEGS_FEET` filter.  
`neck_circumference` is documented under Head, Face & Neck above but is also returned by `TORSO`.  
The ten dimensions marked (‡) are returned by `FULL_BODY` only.

| API Key | Label | Type | ISO 7250-1:2017 | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `axilla_height` ‡ | Axilla Height | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the floor to the axilla (armpit) in standing position. Used for crutch length determination, vehicle grab-bar and overhead-rail height design, and child safety seat harness buckle positioning. |
| `back_waist_length` | Back Waist Length | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the cervicale (C7 vertebra) to the natural waist line along the spine. | `biacromial_breadth` | Shoulder Breadth (Bony) | BONE | 6.2.7 | Distance between the right and left acromion landmarks (bony shoulder tips). | `bust_circumference` | Bust Circumference | FLESH | — | Horizontal circumference at the level of maximum breast protrusion. Computed for all genders; clinically relevant primarily for female subjects. Basis for bra cup sizing when used with `underbust_circumference`: cup size = `bust_circumference` − `underbust_circumference`. |
| `buttock_circumference` ‡ | Buttock Circumference | FLESH | — | Horizontal circumference at the level of maximum posterior gluteal protrusion, in standing position. | `buttock_depth` ‡ | Buttock Depth | BONE | — | Maximum horizontal depth from the anterior abdominal surface to the posterior gluteal protrusion at hip level. | `buttock_height` ‡ | Buttock Height | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the floor to the maximum posterior protrusion of the buttocks. Defines the height at which hip circumference is maximum. Used in chair backrest height determination and vehicle seat design. |
| `buttock_knee_length` | Buttock-Knee Length | BONE | 6.4.8 | Horizontal distance from the posterior point on the buttocks to the anterior surface of the knee when seated. | `buttock_popliteal_length` | Seat Depth | BONE | 6.4.7 | Horizontal distance from the back of the buttocks to the popliteal surface (back of the lower leg at the knee crease) when seated. | `cervicale_height` | Neck Base Height | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the floor to the 7th cervical vertebra (C7, nape point). Reference height for back length measurements. Together with `shoulder_height`, used to verify bodice and jacket back proportions in pattern making. |
| `chest_breadth` | Chest Breadth | BONE | 6.1.11 | Horizontal distance between the lateral margins of the chest (widest point of rib cage). | `chest_circumference` | Chest Circumference | FLESH | 6.4.10 | Horizontal circumference of the chest measured at nipple level. The standard parameter for upper body clothing sizing globally (shirt, jacket, suit chest size). Typical adult male range: 840–1100 mm; female: 790–1050 mm. Note: `bust_circumference` measures at maximum protrusion and is slightly larger in female subjects. |
| `chest_depth` | Chest Depth | FLESH | 6.1.9 | Horizontal distance between the front and back of the chest at nipple level. Together with `chest_breadth`, defines chest cross-sectional shape. Used in body armour and protective vest depth clearance design, and in medical imaging protocol setup. |
| `chest_height` | Chest Height | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the floor to the nipple level. Reference height for garment front-length and chest pocket positioning. Used to verify bodice and jacket proportions against standardised size charts. |
| `crotch_length_omphalion` ‡ | Crotch Length (Anterior to Navel) | BONE | — | Anterior surface arc from the crotch (perineum) to the navel (omphalion point). | `elbow_rest_height` | Elbow Rest Height | BONE | 6.2.5 | Vertical distance from the seat surface to the underside of the elbow when the arm hangs relaxed. | `eye_height_sitting` | Eye Height (Sitting) | BONE | 6.2.2 | Vertical distance from the seat surface to the inner corner of the eye (endocanthion) when seated upright. Determines screen height placement in cockpits and vehicle cabins, and sightline calculations for seated workstations. |
| `hip_breadth` | Hip Breadth | FLESH | 6.1.12 | Maximum horizontal breadth across the widest part of the hips when standing (bideltoid hip). Soft tissue measurement — includes gluteal muscle. | `hip_breadth_bicristal` | Pelvic Breadth (Bony) | BONE | — | Horizontal distance between the superior-anterior points of the iliac crests (pelvic brim). | `hip_circumference` | Hip Circumference | FLESH | — | Maximum circumference around the buttocks and hips at greatest gluteal protrusion, standing. Critical for lower-body clothing sizing (trousers, skirts, jeans). Typical adult male range: 900–1100 mm; female: 900–1150 mm. Differs from `buttock_circumference` in posture reference point. |
| `interscye` ‡ | Interscye | BONE | — | Surface arc across the back between the two armscye (shoulder-joint) points measured at scapula level. | `interscye_i` ‡ | Interscye I | BONE | — | Variant back-breadth arc between armscye points measured at acromion (shoulder tip) level — slightly narrower than `interscye`. | `knee_height` † | Knee Height | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the floor to the top of the knee cap (tibiale point) in standing position. Used in workspace design to ensure knee clearance under desks and tables, and in vehicle footwell design. Also returned by `LEGS_FEET` filter. | `popliteal_height` † | Popliteal Height | BONE | 6.2.11 | Vertical distance from the floor to the popliteal surface (underside of the knee joint) when seated at 90°. | `shoulder_breadth` | Shoulder Breadth | BONE | 6.2.8 | Bideltoid breadth — horizontal distance between the lateral surfaces of the shoulders (deltoid muscle outer margins). Approximately 25–35 mm wider than `biacromial_breadth`. Standard measurement for clothing shoulder width (jacket, coat), doorway clearance, and workspace lateral reach envelope. |
| `shoulder_circumference` ‡ | Shoulder Circumference | FLESH | — | Horizontal circumference around the body at acromion level, passing over the deltoid muscles. Used in jacket and coat pattern making for armhole depth calculation, and in wetsuit shoulder panel design. |
| `shoulder_height` | Shoulder Height | BONE | 6.1.4 | Vertical distance from the floor to the acromion landmark. Reference height for garment shoulder seam positioning. Together with `cervicale_height`, defines the slope of the shoulder. |
| `shoulder_to_waist_length` | Shoulder to Waist Length | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the acromion to the natural waist line. Defines garment torso length for jacket, dress, and bodice sizing. An alternative to `back_waist_length` measured from shoulder rather than nape. |

| `suprasternale_height` ‡ | Suprasternale Height | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the floor to the suprasternale (jugular notch, the top of the sternum). Marks the front neckline position. Used in neckline and collar design for tailored garments and in sternal-pad positioning for PPE. |

| `trochanterion_height` † | Hip Height | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the floor to the greater trochanter of the femur (lateral hip bone prominence). | `underbust_circumference` | Underbust Circumference | FLESH | — | Horizontal circumference of the torso measured directly under the breasts at the inframammary fold. | `vertical_trunk_circumference` ‡ | Vertical Trunk Circumference | FLESH | — | Full-body vertical girth: tape from the right shoulder (acromion), over the front of the body, through the crotch, and back up to the starting point. | `waist_breadth` | Waist Breadth | FLESH | — | Maximum horizontal breadth of the torso at natural waist level. Together with `waist_depth`, defines the cross-sectional shape of the waist. Used in corset pattern construction and body armour torso panel design. |

| `waist_height_omphalion` | Navel Height | BONE | — | Vertical distance from the floor to the level of the navel. Marks the mid-waist reference point used in low-rise and mid-rise garment design, and as a surface landmark for abdominal depth assessment. |

## Notes

**Body height and body mass** — `body_height` and `body_mass` are input anchor measurements, not predicted output dimensions. If provided as anchors in the request, they are returned in `body_dimensions` with `"type": "MEASURED"` and `confidence_score: 100`, but only when the API has them in its anchor input set.

**Anchor override** — Any dimension supplied as an input anchor (`anchors` field in the request) is returned with `"type": "MEASURED"` and `confidence_score: 100`, regardless of which engine predicted it.

**Confidence Score** — A proprietary heuristic index [0–100] indicating prediction reliability relative to the anchor set provided. It is not a frequentist 95% prediction interval. `range_95` (when requested) provides the statistical interval. BONE dimensions typically score 82–92; FLESH dimensions 72–84. `plantar_arch_height` is an outlier at ~60 due to high population variability.

**Face length aliases** — `face_length` and `menton_sellion_length` are the same measurement (sellion to menton, ISO 6.3.11). Both are returned with equal values. The duplication supports customers using either the ISO anatomical name or the craniometric name.

**Shoulder overlap** — `shoulder_elbow_length` (ISO 6.2.6) and `upper_arm_length` (ISO 6.2.6) are anatomically identical measurements (acromion to radiale). Both are retained as commercial aliases used in different technical standards.

**Waist circumference pair** — `waist_circumference_natural` (narrowest torso point, ISO 6.4.11) and `waist_circumference_omphalion` (navel level) serve different purposes. Use `waist_circumference_natural` for clothing sizing; `waist_circumference_omphalion` for clinical and cardiometabolic risk assessment (NHANES/WHO protocol).

**Inseam aliases** — `inseam_length` and `crotch_height` return equivalent values. `crotch_height` is the ISO engineering term; `inseam_length` is the apparel industry term.

**Bridge width derivation** — `bridge_width` is derived from `nasal_root_breadth` with a −4 mm correction (standard 2 mm per nose pad contact point). `nasal_root_breadth` is the raw anatomical value for applications where physical nose-pad contact is not assumed (AR/VR nose rests, face scan alignment).

**Digit width derivation** — All `hand_digit_X_width_dip` and `hand_digit_X_width_pip` values are derived via Greiner validated skeletal proportion constants from `hand_length`. These dimensions carry an approximate −10% confidence adjustment relative to directly modelled dimensions.

**Multi-bundle dimensions** — The following dimensions are returned by two bundle filters:
- `neck_circumference` → `HEAD_FACE` and `TORSO`
- `knee_height` → `TORSO` and `LEGS_FEET`
- `popliteal_height` → `TORSO` and `LEGS_FEET`
- `trochanterion_height` → `TORSO` and `LEGS_FEET`
- `buttock_knee_length` → `TORSO` and `LEGS_FEET`
- `buttock_popliteal_length` → `TORSO` and `LEGS_FEET`

**ISO 7250-1:2017** — ISO codes follow the 2017 revision. `—` indicates no standardised code for that dimension in the current revision. ISO codes are omitted from the response when `include_iso_codes: false` is set in `output_format`.
